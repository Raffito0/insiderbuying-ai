- **Reason**: Buying domains requires capital ($8-15/domain minimum). Selling requires marketplace presence and reputation. Time to first sale is typically weeks-months. Not compatible with EUR 100 budget or 14-day revenue timeline.

### Killed: Prediction Market Arbitrage Bot
- **Reason**: Median arbitrage spread is 0.3% in 2026 ([market data](https://www.financemagnates.com/trending/prediction-markets-are-turning-into-a-bot-playground/)). Combined fees of 5%+ make most opportunities fee-negative. 73% of profits go to sub-100ms bots -- our VPS cannot compete on speed. Requires significant capital ($1K+) to generate meaningful returns.

### Killed: Automated Cold Email B2B Lead Gen
- **Reason**: Already failed per user context. Email deliverability requires expensive infrastructure. GDPR compliance in EU makes cold B2B email legally risky.

### Killed: AI Content Writing Service
- **Reason**: Already failed per user context. AI detection is increasingly effective. Race to the bottom on pricing. No sustainable moat.

### Killed: Dropshipping / Print-on-Demand Automation
- **Reason**: Requires advertising budget (Facebook/Google Ads). Customer acquisition cost typically $15-30 per customer. Customer service is intensive. Returns handling. Not compatible with "zero manual work" constraint.

- **Reason**: Gumroad is not a marketplace -- zero organic traffic ([Kupkaike research](https://kupkaike.com/blog/how-to-sell-notion-templates-gumroad-2026)). 99.5% of revenue goes to 1% of sellers. - **Reason**: Anti-bot measures are specifically designed for this use case. Legal gray area (circumventing purchase limits). Requires very fast execution (items sell out in <10 seconds). Our VPS cannot compete with dedicated sneaker bot infrastructure.

### Killed: Google Business Profile Management Service
- **Reason**: Requires access to client's GBP account (trust barrier). Semrush and other major players offer this already. Manual onboarding for each client. Not compatible with "zero manual work" after setup.

### Killed: Automated Review Response Service
- **Reason**: Requires access to client's review platforms (Google, Yelp, etc.). Each platform has different API requirements and ToS. Risk of generating inappropriate automated responses. Heavy customer service burden. Major players (BirdEye, Podium) dominate.
