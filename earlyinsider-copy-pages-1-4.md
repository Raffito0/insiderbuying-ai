# PAGE 4: HOW IT WORKS (/how-it-works)

---

## Section 1: Hero

**Headline**:
150,000+ Form 4 Filings Per Year. Most Investors See Them Late.

**Problem statement**:
The SEC publishes insider trading disclosures on EDGAR within hours of the transaction. The data is public. The format is inconsistent. The volume is unmanageable. And 80% of filings are routine noise -- compensation grants, pre-scheduled 10b5-1 dispositions, tax withholding. The 20% that represent genuine conviction buying are buried in the same feed.

The average retail investor encounters this data 24-48 hours after filing, via a newsletter or social media post. By then, 25% of the abnormal return has already accrued.

---

## Section 2: Data Pipeline -- 3 Steps

**Section header**:
The Data Pipeline

---

**Step 1: Ingest**
Continuous monitoring of SEC EDGAR across 17,325+ public companies. Every Form 3, Form 4, and Schedule 13D is captured within seconds of publication. Raw XML parsed into structured data: insider identity, transaction type, share count, price, ownership change, and filing timestamp.

**Step 2: Analyze**
Each filing passes through a proprietary scoring engine that evaluates 6 dimensions: trade size relative to the insider's 12-month average, open-market purchase vs. compensation grant, executive seniority, 5-year track record of the insider's prior trades, cluster activity (multiple insiders within 14 days), and sector context. Filings scoring above the conviction threshold receive a detailed plain-English explanation.

**Step 3: Deliver**
Scored alerts are delivered via email, SMS, webhook, or API. Average pipeline latency from EDGAR publication to subscriber inbox: under 60 seconds. Filings are simultaneously indexed against 94M+ historical records for cross-reference and trend detection.

## Section 4: Technology Stack

**Section header**:
Analysis Infrastructure

Proprietary analysis engine built on three layers:

**Layer 1 -- Filing Intelligence**
Automated parsing of SEC EDGAR XML into structured transaction records. Pattern recognition across 94M+ indexed filings dating to 2004. Anomaly detection flags filings that deviate from an insider's established trading pattern.

**Layer 2 -- Conviction Scoring**
Multi-factor model scoring each filing on a 0-100 scale. Factors include trade magnitude, insider history, cluster signals, compensation context, and sector-relative benchmarks. Scores above 70 are classified as high-conviction.

**Layer 3 -- Contextual Synthesis**
Each high-conviction filing receives a plain-English analysis: what happened, why it matters relative to the company's recent trajectory, and what conditions would change the thesis. No jargon. No hedging. Explicit analytical position on every scored filing.

**Section header**:
Historical Validation

94M+ SEC filing records indexed. Backtesting against 10 years of Form 4 data (2015-2025) validates the conviction scoring methodology.
