The 20% that signal genuine conviction reach most retail investors 24-48 hours after the filing, well past the 5-day window where 25% of abnormal returns accrue.

---

## The Solution

EarlyInsider monitors SEC EDGAR continuously across 17,325+ companies and parses every Form 3, 4, and 13D filing within seconds of publication. Each filing runs through a conviction-scoring model that weighs trade size against historical averages, cross-references executive track record, and detects cluster buying patterns across the C-suite. The result is a structured alert -- filing data, conviction score, plain-English context -- delivered to subscribers in under 60 seconds.

Every data point on EarlyInsider traces back to an SEC filing or a verified financial dataset. Stating what the system cannot do is as important as stating what it can.

### How EarlyInsider Analyzes Insider Trading Data

Every conviction score, every alert, and every research report produced by EarlyInsider follows a documented, reproducible process. This page describes the data sources, analysis pipeline, and known limitations of the platform. 94M+ records indexed. 17,325+ companies monitored. Zero black boxes.

## Data Sources

**1. SEC EDGAR -- Form 3, Form 4, Form 5, Schedule 13D/13G**

Primary source for all insider transaction data. EarlyInsider polls SEC EDGAR's full-text search index and XBRL feeds continuously. Every filing is linked directly to its SEC EDGAR URL for independent verification. Coverage spans 17,325+ public companies across NYSE, NASDAQ, and OTC markets.

**2. Market Data -- Price, Volume, Fundamentals**

Real-time and historical pricing data sourced from licensed market data providers. Used to contextualize insider trades: price at time of filing, 30/60/90-day price action, average daily volume, market capitalization, P/E, and free cash flow. 94M+ data points indexed across 10+ years of market history.

**3. Executive and Corporate Records**

Officer and director histories, compensation data, prior Form 4 filing track records, and corporate event timelines (earnings dates, M&A announcements, dividend declarations). Used to build executive track records and detect cluster buying patterns across the C-suite and board.

---

## Analysis Process

**Step 1: Ingestion and Parsing**

SEC EDGAR filings are detected within seconds of publication. Each filing is parsed into structured data: insider identity, title, transaction type, share count, price per share, total value, ownership change, and direct vs. indirect holdings. Automated validation flags formatting anomalies and incomplete filings for secondary review.

**Step 2: Noise Filtering**

80% of Form 4 filings are routine: scheduled 10b5-1 plan executions, small option exercises, and automatic share withholding for tax obligations. The filtering engine classifies each transaction against 14 criteria -- including transaction code, trade size relative to holdings, filing timing relative to earnings, and 10b5-1 plan indicators -- to isolate the 20% that reflect discretionary conviction.

**Step 3: Conviction Scoring**

Each filing that passes the noise filter receives a conviction score from 0 to 100. The scoring model weighs 7 factors: trade size vs. 3-year historical average for that insider, executive seniority and track record, cluster activity (multiple insiders buying within a 14-day window), trade timing relative to earnings and corporate events, insider's historical hit rate on prior purchases, sector momentum context, and purchase-to-holdings ratio. Scores above 75 are classified as high-conviction signals.

**Step 4: Contextual Analysis and Delivery**

High-scoring filings receive a plain-English analysis: what the insider bought, why it matters relative to their history, whether other insiders at the same company have filed recently, and what market conditions surround the trade. The complete alert -- structured data, conviction score, and narrative context -- is delivered to subscribers via email, Slack, or webhook in under 60 seconds from the original SEC filing timestamp.

---

## AI Transparency

The analysis engine uses large language models to generate plain-English filing context, research report narratives, and conviction score explanations. The models operate exclusively on structured data extracted from SEC filings and verified financial datasets. No model has access to material nonpublic information. No model executes trades or generates buy/sell recommendations.

Every AI-generated narrative is constrained by the underlying structured data. If the filing data says the CEO purchased 50,000 shares at $34.12, the narrative cannot state a different figure. Conviction scores are computed algorithmically from the 7 weighted factors described above; the language model explains the score but does not determine it.

---

## Limitations

**Mandatory reporting lag.** SEC Form 4 filings must be submitted within 2 business days of the transaction. EarlyInsider detects filings within seconds of their SEC EDGAR publication, but the underlying trade may have occurred up to 48 hours earlier. Price movement between transaction date and filing date is outside the platform's control.

**10b5-1 pre-planned trades.** Insiders who file trades under Rule 10b5-1 plans execute on a predetermined schedule, not in response to current information. The noise filter flags known 10b5-1 transactions, but not all plans are publicly disclosed at the time of the trade. Some routine transactions may receive higher conviction scores than warranted.

**AI analysis scope.** The analysis engine operates on public data only: SEC filings, market prices, historical fundamentals, and corporate event calendars. It cannot assess management sentiment from private communications, board-level strategy discussions, or pending regulatory actions. Analysis quality is bounded by the completeness of public disclosure.

**Historical performance is not predictive.** Academic research (Jeng, Metrick & Zeckhauser, 1999; Lakonishok & Lee, 1998) demonstrates that insider purchases have historically outperformed market benchmarks. Past patterns in aggregate insider trading data do not guarantee future results for any individual filing or trade.
