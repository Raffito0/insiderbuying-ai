### What You Will Learn

**Section 1:**
**The 23.4% Edge: Where It Comes From and Where It Fails**
CEO purchases outperformed the S&P 500 by 23.4% on average across 50,247 filings. The effect was strongest in companies under $2B market cap and weakest in mega-cap stocks above $50B. The report breaks down returns by market cap decile, sector, and insider role.

**Section 2:**
**The 5-Day Window: 25% of Returns Accrue Before Most Investors React**
Jeng, Metrick & Zeckhauser (Harvard/Wharton, 1999) found that 25% of abnormal returns from insider purchases materialize within the first 5 trading days. The report maps this decay curve against current SEC filing-to-publication latency and quantifies the cost of a 24-hour delay.

**Section 3:**
**Noise vs. Signal: Why 80% of Form 4 Filings Are Uninformative**
Not all insider purchases carry equal weight. 10b5-1 pre-planned trades, option exercises, and small acquisitions below $50K account for roughly 80% of filings. The report details the 7 filters that isolate high-conviction purchases from routine transactions.
